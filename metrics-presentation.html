Metrics & Measurement Framework

Prepared for leadership review · 10 metric categories · Phase 1 already instrumented
01

Our measurement philosophy

Every number answers one of four questions. If a metric doesn't, we don't track it.

1

Are people showing up?

Growth & activation — signups, and whether they reach the first real interview.

2

Are they using it?

Engagement — active users, interviews run, depth and cadence of practice.

3

Is it making them better?

Outcome — the readiness score rising over time. This is our differentiator.

4

Is it a real business?

Cost & monetization — unit economics, conversion, and revenue as we scale.

We stack metrics in three layers so we never drown in numbers — a single North Star, one or two headline KPIs per category, and the operational detail we drill into only when a KPI moves.

02

The North Star

The one number that captures value

Weekly interviews completed that raise a user's Interview Readiness.

It only goes up if people come back, finish, and actually improve.

It fuses engagement and outcome into one honest figure. We protect it with three guardrail counter-metrics — completion rate, cost per interview, and satisfaction — so the North Star can never be gamed at the expense of quality.
